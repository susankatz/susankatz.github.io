    So, what can we conclude from this data?

  1. Across all price ranges, Austria's average wine rating was amongnst the top scoring countries
  2. If you're looking to buy a wine at the $500+ price point, Wine Enthusiast's connoiseurs found that Australian and French wines were on average the best
  3. Need to impressive your guests at a dinner party? You can get a bottle of wine for $25-$50 from Germany or Portugal. On average, these two countries produce the best wines at this price range.
  4. Want a bottle of wine for under $25? Indian wines, on average, score the highest in this price range.

    Things to keep in mind when using this tool fo analysis:

  1. This data accounts for all varieties of wine, so averages can differ when searching for a specific type of wine
  2. The average wine scores do not account for the varying quantities of samples taken per country

Wine Enthusiast is a reputable magazine and brand that is known for their yearly reviews on over 24,000 wines across the globe. In each wine in review, their taste specialists provides descriptive flavor profiling and documentation of the products' price, grape variety, and place of orgin. These data points are then ultimately used to determine the wine's "scoring". This experience focuses on the 2017 wine reviews.
